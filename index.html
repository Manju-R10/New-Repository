Kalpana Chawla

Indian-American astronaut and engineer who was the first woman of Indian origin to fly in space.

About the Legend

Kalpana Chawla was born on March 17, 1962, in Karnal, Haryana, India. She earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from Punjab Engineering College and later moved to the United States, where she obtained her master's degree from the University of Texas and her Ph.D. from the University of Colorado.

Chawla joined NASA in 1994 and flew on two Space Shuttle missions, STS-87 and STS-107. She was the first Indian-born woman in space and spent over 30 days in space during her two missions.

Early Life and Education

Kalpana Chawla was born in Karnal, Haryana, India, to Banarasi Lal Chawla and Sanyogita Devi. She had two siblings, Sunita and Deepa. Chawla's interest in flying was sparked at a young age, and she began flying lessons at the Flying Club of Punjab.

Career

Chawla earned her bachelor's degree in aeronautical engineering from Punjab Engineering College in 1982. She then moved to the United States, where she earned her master's degree in aerospace engineering from the University of Texas in 1984. Chawla later earned her Ph.D. in aerospace engineering from the University of Colorado in 1988.

Tragic End and Legacy

On February 1, 2003, while re-entering Earth's atmosphere, the Space Shuttle Columbia disintegrated, tragically taking the lives of all seven crew members, including Kalpana Chawla. Her legacy lives on as an inspiration for aspiring astronauts and engineers around the world.

Honors and Recognition

In honor of Kalpana Chawla, NASA named a supercomputer, a satellite, and even an asteroid after her. Numerous scholarships and institutions worldwide bear her name, ensuring that her contribution to space exploration is never forgotten.
